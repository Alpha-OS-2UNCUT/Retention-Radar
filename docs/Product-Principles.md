# Retention Radar Product Principles

## Purpose
These principles guide every product, design and engineering decision. If a proposed feature conflicts with these principles, it should be reconsidered.

## 1. Decision-first, not data-first
The product exists to help users decide what to do next, not to display more dashboards.

## 2. Never present a problem without a next action
Every alert should explain why it matters and recommend a practical next step.

## 3. Augment human judgement
Retention Radar supports business owners with organised, consistent analysis. Final decisions always remain with the user.

## 4. Reduce cognitive load
Move relationship management from memory into a clear visual system.

## 5. Consistency over intensity
Recommendation quality should remain consistent whether a customer manages 10 clients or 1,000.

## 6. Relationship clarity creates retention
Retention is the outcome. Relationship clarity is the mechanism.

## 7. Calm over anxiety
The first experience each day should create clarity and confidence, not panic.

## 8. Simplicity beats feature count
Every feature must justify its existence by making retention easier or decision-making simpler.

## 9. Validate before automating
Prefer learning from real customers before investing in complex engineering.

## 10. AI should feel like a trusted analyst
The AI should organise complexity, identify patterns, explain reasoning and recommend actions without pretending to be infallible.

## North Star Question
Every time a user opens Retention Radar, the product should answer:

1. What should I do next?
2. Who needs me today?
