# Retention Radar User Journey

## Goal
Design the complete customer experience from discovering Retention Radar to becoming a long-term customer.

## Stage 1 — Discovery
The business owner discovers Retention Radar through content, referrals or outreach.

Desired emotion: Curiosity.
Question: 'Could this stop clients slipping through the cracks?'

## Stage 2 — First Impression
The landing page communicates:
- Who RR is for.
- The problem it solves.
- Why it is different.
- A clear call to action.

Desired emotion: Confidence.

## Stage 3 — Research Call
The founder understands the customer's workflow, validates pain points and demonstrates the concept.

Desired emotion: 'These people understand my business.'

## Stage 4 — Onboarding
The customer creates their workspace and adds their clients.

Goal: Reach value in under 10 minutes.

## Stage 5 — Relationship Mapping
Instead of seeing spreadsheets, the customer sees an organised visual map of every important client relationship.

Desired emotion: Clarity.

## Stage 6 — Morning Workspace
Every morning RR answers two questions:
1. What should I do next?
2. Who needs me today?

The experience should feel calm, organised and actionable.

## Stage 7 — Taking Action
Every recommendation includes:
- Why this client was prioritised.
- Recommended next action.
- Expected benefit.

RR never presents problems without suggested actions.

## Stage 8 — Weekly Reflection
The owner reviews completed actions, relationship changes and remaining priorities.

Desired emotion: Control.

## Stage 9 — Long-Term Outcome
Over months the owner notices:
- Fewer forgotten clients.
- Better organised relationships.
- More consistent follow-up.
- Improved retention.
- Increased confidence in daily decision-making.

## North Star Experience
The customer should finish every session feeling more organised and more confident than when they opened the app.
